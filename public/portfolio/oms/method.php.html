Our Methodology

The O&M programs offered at Orientation and Mobility Services of Texas, Inc. are flexible and tailored to fit each client’s specific needs. The O&M services we offer include teaching the specific skills required of a person to travel safely and independently without using sight as their primary sensory source.

Training Areas

The Certified Orientation & Mobility Specialists (C.O.M.S.) employed by O&M Services of Texas, Inc. are qualified to provide training in the following areas:

  • Basic maneuvering skills, including

    • ascending and descending steps
    • escalator and elevator travel
    • business, mall, and store travel
    • use of public transportation systems

  • Sighted guide techniques

  • Cane skills, including

    • use of a long mobility cane
    • touch and diagonal techniques
    • learning the “language” of the cane (knowing what the cane has located and what action the person should then take in order to maneuver around the obstacle they have been presented.)

  • Acclimation to a dog guide

    Involves traveling to areas that are of interest to the client, while conditioning the dog to the various routes and any obstacles the person and their dog may find. We train the owners, but not the dog guides themselves.

  • Use of electronic mobility devices

    • Electronic mobility devices such as a talking compass, laser cane, radar type equipment and other such supplies are available for use. These items are less popular than canes or dog guides because they are often much more expensive, bulky, and mentally intensive than other options.

  • Use of mental skills and other sensory agents available to a person, such as hearing and touch
