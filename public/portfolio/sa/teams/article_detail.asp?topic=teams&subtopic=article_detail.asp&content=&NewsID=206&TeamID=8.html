91 Girls: 2003 Austin Cup-Champions in Top Open Division

Tuesday, May 27, 2003
 
The 91 Girls traveled down to Austin, Texas for the prestigious Austin Cup over the Memorial Day weekend. The girls, their brothers and sisters, parents and the coach had a great in Austin with swimming at Barton Spring and lots and lots of good eating and spending time just having fun. And yes, a little soccer was played along the way.

Things did not start well for the girls, the first game ended in zero to zero tie and after about ten minutes into the second game, the team found themselves down 2 to 0. Then before halftime, Brittany scored on a beautiful shot and the girls started the second half down 2-1. The 91 team took control of the second half and, with true team play Sancia was able to finish on two goals. The 3-2 come from behind win charged up the Soccer America girls for the rest of the tournament play.

The back line of Alex, Loren and Kristin was very strong, with the all the opposing teams playing a well-coordinated attack. Time after time the defense was tested with well placed through balls, size and speed and the defense met the challenges with determination, strength and smart play. The second day of play saw the girls win both games and advance the finals.

In the finals, the girls found themselves again down, this time 1 to 0. With about ten minutes left in regulation things were starting to get tense. Then the SA team was awarded a direct kick about 25 yards outside the box. Molly stepped up to the challenge and struck a awesome shot that found net pass a diving goalkeeper.

After the overtime, the score remained 1 to 1 and it was time for penalty kicks to determine the winner. Ashley confidently trotted to the goal area to face the series of penalty kicks. After two blocked shots for Ashley, the result was the Soccer America 91 Girls won the championship.

Congratulation Soccer America for taking the top Open Division in the 2003 Austin United Spring Cup. Way to go girls!
