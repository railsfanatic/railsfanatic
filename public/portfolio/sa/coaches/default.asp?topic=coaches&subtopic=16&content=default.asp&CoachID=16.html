Coaching Background

Coach Jacobs has an extensive background in soccer spanning over 32 years as a player and coach. His experiences, which have taken him across the U.S. and to Europe, include playing on North Dallas Chamber of Commerce league championship teams, a U14 state champion, Southern Regionals and a U16 NTSSA European tour, serving as an equipment manager for the Dallas Tornado, coaching high school and club teams and at the SMU and UT camps.

This is Robert’s 12th season coaching high school soccer. Coach Jacobs holds a "C" national coaching license from the U.S.S.F. and a National Diploma from the NSCAA. He was the Competition Coordinator for the ’93 CONCACAF Gold Cup and in 1994 had the honor of serving on the World Cup staff as Assistant Competition Manager for the Dallas venue.

A Texan most of his life, Robert graduated from Eastern Hills High School in Fort Worth. He received a BBA degree in Marketing from TCU. In 1987, as Assistant Promotion Director at KISS 106 FM, he created and organized Dallas Sidekicks Day, the championship celebration that included a mayoral proclamation, downtown parade and a Reunion Arena pep rally.

With a career change, he earned Secondary Teacher Certification in Social Science from UTD and a Master of Education degree in Sports Administration from UT Austin. Coach Jacobs has been recognized in Who’s Who Among America’s Teachers. He recently, he completed the Mid-Management certification program at UTA.

Robert is actively involved in the Texas Association of Soccer Coaches. He has coached in Senior Showcase games and was the director for the event in 2000. Coach Jacobs has also served as Region II Director for TASCO.
