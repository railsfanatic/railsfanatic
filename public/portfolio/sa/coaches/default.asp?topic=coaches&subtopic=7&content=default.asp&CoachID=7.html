Coaching Background

Professional Background

Dalton taught Pre-Advanced Placement Science I & II at West Junior High Technology Magnet in Richardson for 5 years, where he was named R.I.S.D. New Teacher of the Year, 1995-96. He also has a masters degree in education from UT at Dallas. Presently, he works as a Business Analyst for AdvoCare International. AdvoCare is a nutritional supplement company located in Carrollton.

Coaching Philosophies

Dalton understands and empathizes with the unique challenges that teenagers face. He strongly believes in “building-up” players by utilizing positive reinforcement techniques. Every effort is made to focus on the positive attributes of his athletes, while instruction is given on how to correct technical & tactical errors. This method has been shown to improve the performance level of the athlete.

Soccer Coaching Experience: Dalton's professional coaching began at J.J. Pearce High School in 1996. While there, he served two seasons as the Head Junior Varsity Girls coach. In that time, he was responsible for creating the junior varsity program that had only seven players the first season to an impressive growth of over 40 athletes in his second season. Dalton’s last two years at Pearce were served as the Head Varsity Girls coach. During his tenure, he helped develop Pearce into a state powerhouse. Pearce was consistently ranked in the Texas top 10 throughout the 2000 –2001 season. Dalton now trains Soccer America's older girls groups. He currently holds the United States Soccer Federation National "B" license.
